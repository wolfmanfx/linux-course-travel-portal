# Speaker Script — Linux Foundations

This script is embedded in the final PowerPoint speaker notes and matches all 112 slides. The course is balanced 50% guided teaching and 50% hands-on practice; per-slide and per-lab estimates are facilitation aids, not the public course split.

## Slide 1 — Linux Foundations

Welcome learners and establish the course promise: commands are replaceable, but evidence-based reasoning transfers. Explain the 50/50 balance: half the course builds and demonstrates the model; half puts learners at the keyboard. Ask learners to keep the browser lab and slides visible side by side. Timing: 2 minutes. Sources: course scope and C01.

## Slide 2 — By the end, you can explain state before changing it

Purpose: frame outcomes as operational explanations, not memorized commands. Misconception to surface: successful output from one command is not always sufficient proof. Timing: 3 minutes. Sources: course communication job.

## Slide 3 — Every module closes the same reasoning loop

Walk through the repeated cycle once. Explain that the course is balanced 50/50 between guided teaching and hands-on practice, and that labs arrive immediately after prerequisite theory. Learners will never be assessed on an unexplained core concept. Timing: 2 minutes.

## Slide 4 — Predict first; type second; verify last

Set classroom norms. Use one quick example: a missing path is not repaired by broader permissions. Point out the reset behavior before any learner state exists. Timing: 3 minutes.

## Slide 5 — System boundaries and terminal survival

Purpose: open Module 1 with its operating question and scope. Invite one prediction, but do not evaluate prior knowledge publicly. Transition into the minimum model needed for Lab 1. Timing: 1 minute. Sources: C01, C02, C04, C05, C19.
